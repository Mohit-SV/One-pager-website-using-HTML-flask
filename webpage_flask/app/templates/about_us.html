About us


Molecular recognition by crossed graphene edge electrodes

Analyze the tunneling current through molecules in various media both at normal and cryogenic conditions using edges of two twisted graphene layers as the electrodes to study the behavior of electron transport in single molecules. The aim is to achieve high precision real-time characterization of the molecules. The project involves building as- semblies (preparation of graphene, masking it to avoid current leakage; modifying STM and creating sample holders to work at different conditions and media) and character- izing them to fabricate the best design of the setup, experimenting using thus created setup. The project helps in the development of molecular electronics, bio-sensing and a method for DNA sequencing.

Geodesics in Kerr-Neumann Black Holes

Setup time-like and null geodesics from a modified metric constructed to study their propagation close to ring singularity which captures the changes within the inner hori- zon in the modifed Kerr-Neumann Black Holes; and investigate their behavior by soft- ware simulations.

Linear Contraction Measurement in Aluminum alloy Casting

Simulate heat transfer effects and thermal stresses caused by Aluminum alloy contrac- tion during and after its solidification, and find the raw materials needed and the best design to build an instrument which measures the contraction linearly. Then study the mushy zone strength experimentally using the constructed instrument.
